Christianity

A monotheistic religion based on the life, teachings, and crucifixion of Jesus of Nazareth with Jesus considered as a Messiah and the Son of God. Started in Israel, which is based on the teachings of Jesus of Nazareth. His birth, according to the Bible, is stated to be in a stable within Bethlehem, having been birthed by a young virgin woman known as Mary.

Nature of Ultimate Reality

Foundational truth that shapes all existence. It is rooted in God and the Holy trinity, vital for living a life of faith and purpose.For Christianity, they believe that God is the ultimate reality and having a personal relationship with it would allow them to live a full life and experience true happiness.

Origin and Purpose of the World

The world is viewed as the product of God's creative power, a testament to His wisdom and artistry. From the intricate details of nature to the vastness of the cosmos, everything is seen as a reflection of God's divine attributes. The world, however, is not without its flaws. The entry of sin into creation has resulted in a brokenness, a sense of decay and disorder, and the presence of suffering and evil. This fallen state doesn't negate the inherent goodness of creation, but it highlights its brokenness. The world exists primarily to glorify God, to showcase His power, love, and wisdom. Origin, Nature, and Purpose of Humans

Christianity teaches that the universe was created through love by an intelligent power, namely the God of the Bible. God made humanity in his likeness allowed them to comprehend some of God and his intricate and extensive design. All beings are intended to experience perfect love and justice, which is reflected in God's design or purpose for creation. Humans have a complicated relationship with nature. On one hand, we have the ability to make positive changes and improve the planet. Humans seek to understand their place in the world and find significance in their lives. They can contribute to society by working in important industries like healthcare, education, and energy. Humans are social creatures who crave connection and meaning. They can find fulfillment through personal growth, spiritual fulfillment, and meaningful work. Humans can make a positive impact on the world through their actions. They can seek wisdom and knowledge, and can do the right thing and do good.

Source of Sacred Knowledge

Sacred knowledge is characterized as divine wisdom that fosters religious and moral understanding, guiding individuals toward enlightenment and a deeper connection with the divine. In Christianity, The Bible is considered the primary source of sacred knowledge. Christians believe the Bible contains God's words written down by humans and people viewed The Bible as both a human and divine creation.

Nature and Means of Salvation

Christians believe that accepting Jesus as Lord and Savior is the way to experience this salvation. Furthermore, humans respond to it by having faith, repentance, gratitude, a desire to follow God's commands, and a commitment to live a life dedicated to serving others; specifically, recognizing their need for salvation and receiving Jesus Christ as their savior, leads to an entirely new life based on love and obedience to God.

Ethics and Morality

Christian ethics are moral principles based on the Bible, particularly the teachings of Jesus, the Old Testament prophets, and the New Testament apostles. These ethics guide believers in how they should live, emphasizing love for God and others as the highest moral duties. Scripture is the ultimate standard for Christian ethics because God, as the source of goodness, reveals moral truth through His Word. While general revelation (nature and conscience) provides some moral knowledge, it is incomplete without Scripture.Christians are not directly under the Mosaic Law, as Jesus inaugurated a new covenant. However, many laws (e.g., prohibitions against murder and theft) still apply because they reflect God's eternal moral character. Some laws require cultural adaptation, focusing on their underlying ethical principles rather than literal application. Secular ethical systems (e.g., consequentialism, Kantian ethics, virtue ethics) offer insights but must align with biblical truth. Christian ethics assess actions based on God's character, moral duty, and potential consequences, emphasizing both righteousness and virtue. Christians face contemporary ethical challenges (e.g., abortion, same-sex marriage, technology-related moral issues). Even when the Bible does not address a specific topic, biblical principles help guide moral decisions.

Nature and Purpose of its Community

Church for others means a physical building but for Christianity it is the society and fellowship. It is endowed with a profound spiritual reality. Doctrinal debates about the true nature of Jesus and his relationship to God threatened to divide the church. Despite efforts to preserve unity of churches it still separated into two distinct churches: the eastern Church, based in Constantinople, and the western Church, based in Rome, and later in the West, the Protestant churches.

Nature of the Afterlife

Afterlife means what happens to our soul after we die. In Christianity including Catholics, Protestants, and other Christian groups there are five main beliefs about the afterlife: Heaven, Hell, Judgment, Purgatory, and Resurrection. Resurrection is connected to Final Judgment. Christians believe that in the end, God will create a new Heaven and Earth, and people will be resurrected with perfect bodies in a perfect world.
